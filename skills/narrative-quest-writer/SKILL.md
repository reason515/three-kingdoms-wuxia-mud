# Narrative Quest Writer

## Purpose

Create compact historical-wuxia scenarios in which gameplay and narrative reinforce each other: information changes judgment, abilities unlock methods, choices impose costs, relationships alter later events, and repeated runs reveal genuinely new facts.

## Narrative style

- Tone: late-Han turmoil, jianghu oaths, restrained melancholy, human courage with costs.
- Use historical turbulence as pressure, not as a lecture.
- Prefer common people, minor officers, couriers, physicians, escorts, refugees and nameless disciples as the player's immediate world.
- Let historical figures retain political motives and independent agency.
- Write scenes and combat with concrete space, movement and sensory detail in the concise tradition of text MUDs.
- Use reference works for high-level techniques only; never copy their plots, organizations, characters or signature language.

## Scenario structure

Design with **branch-and-foldback** rather than an endlessly expanding tree:

1. Establish an identity, mission and incomplete understanding.
2. Present 2–3 local routes with real opportunity costs.
3. Fold routes into a shared historical pressure point.
4. Let accumulated state substantially change available actions and human outcomes.
5. Resolve the fixed historical anchor while varying hidden causes, evidence, reputations and personal fates.
6. Reveal unanswered questions that another identity or route can investigate.

## Choice standard

Every choice should change mechanics, knowledge or future content. Strong choices usually trade among:

- mission and human life;
- truth and trust;
- speed and safety;
- loyalty and survival;
- evidence and reputation;
- short-term advantage and long-term consequence.

Do not make every click a life-or-death climax. Instead, keep consequence density high and place major irreversible commitments roughly every 3–5 actions.

Give players enough evidence to understand the type of risk, while preserving uncertainty about exact outcomes. Avoid arbitrary gotchas.

## Required output

For each scenario or quest, include:

1. **Premise and player identity**
2. **Historical anchors and change boundaries**
3. **Initial belief and hidden truth**
4. **Key characters, motives and private costs**
5. **Scarce resources and relevant abilities**
6. **Scene or event beats**
7. **Choices with conditions, gains, costs and delayed consequences**
8. **Foldback points and state-dependent variations**
9. **Evidence and conflicting accounts**
10. **Main ending, character fates and jianghu commentary**
11. **What a later run can newly reveal**
12. **Required locations, items, combat and data flags**
13. **Reusable prose snippets and localization notes**

## Replay quality check

A second route should provide at least one of the following, not merely alternate wording:

- a new fact;
- a different causal explanation;
- a new relationship context;
- a mechanically distinct solution;
- a different person's fate;
- evidence that challenges a prior conclusion.

## Avoid

- Long uninteractive cutscenes.
- Choices that converge without meaningful state differences.
- Perfect routes that save everyone without exceptional cost.
- Famous-character fan service without player agency.
- Mystery answers that depend on information never available to the player.
